GOAL (bounded proof-of-concept, NOT a full rollout): prove whether faithfully assembling a LobCorp character from the raw-extracted parts is achievable, by getting ONE character — 'Standard Agent' — to render as a recognizable little face+body, not a blob. If it works, we templatize across all 10 in a follow-up ticket; if it can't be made to look right, report that honestly so we change strategy.

WHY THIS IS NEEDED: compose_character currently blits full-resolution part crops onto the 32x40 canvas with NO per-layer scale, so parts overflow and read as blobs. Worse, the parts do NOT share a coordinate system — measured native sizes of Standard Agent's parts:
- Head 193x200, FrontHair(v0) and RearHair(v0) ~200-275px tall, Clothes(Agent idx0 torso) 66x94, Eyes(Default v0) 23x26, EyeBrow_1 v0 19x7, Mouth_1 v0 11x9, skeleton2 limb ~16x11, skeleton torso idx0 25x33.
So a single global downscale won't work; each layer needs its own scale (and the existing LAYER_OFFSETS, authored for a 32px canvas, likely need to be re-derived for whatever canvas/approach you choose).

SUGGESTED APPROACH (use judgment, you may deviate if something works better):
1. Composite on a larger working canvas (e.g. 128 or 256 px) so parts have room, then downscale the finished composite to (SPRITE_W, SPRITE_H)=32x40 as the final step (the engine contract requires every clip frame be exactly that size — keep that).
2. Introduce a per-layer SCALE in settings.py (e.g. LAYER_SCALES dict parallel to LAYER_OFFSETS) and have compose_character scale each part to a target before placing it. Re-anchor using offsets expressed in the working-canvas coordinate space.
3. Hand-tune Standard Agent's per-layer scales + offsets VISUALLY: dump idle frame 0 (and ideally the layered build-up) to /tmp PNGs, READ them back with your image tools, and iterate until head/eyes/brows/mouth/hair/clothes sit in believable proportion and position. Aim for a clear face: head as the base, eyes+brows+mouth correctly placed and sized on it, hair framing it, body/clothes below.

SCOPE GUARDRAILS:
- Only Standard Agent must look right. The other 9 may still be rough — that's fine; do NOT spend time tuning them.
- Keep the graceful fallback fully intact (missing assets / load errors -> placeholder, never crash).
- Full `uv run --project . pytest` must stay green (size contract holds; no real assets required for the suite). 
- Touch ONLY lobcorp_renderer.py and settings.py. Do not change provider.py, character_defs.py, world.py.

DELIVERABLE + REPORT (in your completion note): the calibrated per-layer scale/offset values for Standard Agent; an honest assessment of how good it looks (attach what you observed from the PNGs); and whether the same scale template is likely to work for the other 9 or whether each needs bespoke tuning. This verdict drives the next ticket.
