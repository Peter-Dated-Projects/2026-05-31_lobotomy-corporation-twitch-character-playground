RESEARCH ONLY — do not write any code. Use WebSearch/WebFetch (load them via ToolSearch) to investigate how game developers make NPC locomotion look natural, then write a structured brief to docs/research/npc-movement-steering.md and commit it. Cite sources (titles + URLs).

INVESTIGATE:
- Craig Reynolds steering behaviors: seek, flee, arrive, wander, pursuit/evade, and especially the canonical WANDER behavior (steering force toward a point on a projected circle ahead of the agent) that produces smooth, non-jittery meandering — vs naive random direction flips which look robotic.
- Velocity-based facing/orientation: how agents face their direction of travel (and for 2D side-scrollers, horizontal sprite flipping by sign of velocity.x). Note: OUR characters currently track a _facing field in sim but the renderer never flips the sprite — call out the standard fix.
- Acceleration/easing, max-force vs max-speed, arrival slowdown, so starts/stops read naturally instead of teleporting between speeds.
- Idle/wander micro-behaviors that add life (pauses, look-around, weight shifts) and how they're scheduled.
- Constraints of a LOW-FPS stop-motion look (our render is ~12fps, anim ~8fps, delta-time integration): what still reads well at low frame rates.

APPLY TO OUR PROJECT (read first for grounding): twitch_playground/sim/steering.py, twitch_playground/sim/character.py, twitch_playground/settings.py, docs/design/sidescroller-contract.md. Characters stroll on horizontal platforms, occasionally jump, in a side view.

DELIVER: docs/research/npc-movement-steering.md with: (1) summary of techniques, (2) concrete recommendations mapped to our steering.py/character.py (what to add/change, e.g. a real wander steering force, velocity-driven facing + renderer flip, acceleration easing), (3) cited sources. Keep it actionable for an implementer; flag the cheapest high-impact wins.
