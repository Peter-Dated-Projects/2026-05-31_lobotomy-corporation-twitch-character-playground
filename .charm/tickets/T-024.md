INVESTIGATE:
- Computational emotion/affect models: OCC (appraisal) model, PAD (Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance) dimensional model, mood vs transient emotion, decay over time.
- Emotion state machines in games: how a small set of emotional states is entered/exited from events and influences both BEHAVIOR (movement speed, jitteriness, willingness to socialize) and ANIMATION (facial expression, posture).
- Emotional contagion / mood spread through a crowd: how one agent's emotion propagates to neighbors (a key 'crowd consciousness' mechanic) — models and tunable spread/decay.
- Keeping it cheap and legible at small scale (a Twitch-stream crowd of chibi characters), not an academic affective-computing system.

APPLY TO OUR PROJECT (read first): our renderer already supports emotion-keyed faces with states 'default'/'battle'/'panic' (see .charm/kb/architecture/character-layer-stack.md and twitch_playground/assets/lobcorp_renderer.py compose_character emotion arg + character_defs eyes/eyebrows/mouth dicts). twitch_playground/sim/character.py has an EMOTING mode (currently just the hug clip). Chat commands could trigger emotions. We want to actually USE the default/battle/panic faces driven by an emotion model, and let emotion affect movement.

DELIVER: docs/research/npc-emotion-models.md with (1) techniques summary, (2) concrete recommendations: a minimal per-character emotion state (mapping to our existing default/battle/panic faces), what events drive transitions (chat commands, proximity, contagion from neighbors), how emotion modulates movement, and how it plugs into character.py's update + the renderer's emotion arg, (3) cited sources.
