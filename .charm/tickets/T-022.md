INVESTIGATE:
- Boids / flocking (Reynolds): separation, alignment, cohesion — how three local rules produce emergent crowd motion, and the weights/radii that tune it.
- Social Force Model (Helbing & Molnar): agents as particles with attractive/repulsive social forces, personal space, density response — how a crowd member 'senses' and reacts to those around it.
- Perception/awareness: how an agent becomes 'conscious of the crowd it's in' — neighbor queries, local density, field-of-view, and doing it EFFICIENTLY (spatial hashing / grids) for many agents.
- Emergent behaviors: lane formation, clustering, milling, avoidance — what produces a lively-but-not-chaotic crowd.
- What scales down to a modest 2D pygame side-scroller with up to ~100 characters on platforms (our MAX_CHARACTERS=100).

APPLY TO OUR PROJECT (read first): twitch_playground/sim/world.py (already computes neighbor_positions + has horizontal-separation settings HSEP_*), twitch_playground/sim/character.py, twitch_playground/sim/steering.py, twitch_playground/settings.py. We already have a basic horizontal separation push; this research should show how to evolve it into genuine crowd awareness (cohesion/alignment, density sensing).

DELIVER: docs/research/crowd-awareness-simulation.md with (1) techniques summary, (2) concrete recommendations mapped to our world.py/character.py (e.g. add cohesion/alignment to the existing separation, a cheap neighbor/density query, tunable weights in settings), (3) cited sources. Note performance implications at N=100.
